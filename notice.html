Notice & Takedown Policy

Noctua Forest respects intellectual property rights and complies with the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA) and other applicable laws. This policy outlines our procedures for addressing copyright infringement claims and other content removal requests.

DMCA Notices

To file a DMCA takedown notice, please provide:

  • Identification of the copyrighted work claimed to be infringed
  • Identification of the material claimed to be infringing
  • Your contact information
  • A statement of good faith belief that the use is not authorized
  • A statement that the information is accurate under penalty of perjury
  • Your physical or electronic signature

Fair Use

We respect fair use principles under copyright law. Book reviews, commentary, and criticism may qualify as fair use. We evaluate each takedown request considering these factors:

  • Purpose and character of the use
  • Nature of the copyrighted work
  • Amount and substantiality of the portion used
  • Effect on the potential market

Content Removal Requests

For non-copyright content removal requests:

  • Identify the specific content you want removed
  • Explain why the content should be removed
  • Provide your contact information
  • Include any relevant documentation

Our Process

  1. 1. We acknowledge receipt of valid notices within 2 business days
  2. 2. We review the claim and supporting evidence
  3. 3. We may contact involved parties for additional information
  4. 4. We take appropriate action based on our review
  5. 5. We notify relevant parties of our decision

Counter Notices

If you believe your content was removed in error, you may submit a counter notice including:

  • Identification of the removed content
  • A statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good faith belief the material was removed by mistake
  • Your contact information
  • Consent to local federal court jurisdiction

Repeat Infringers

We maintain a policy to terminate accounts of users who repeatedly infringe or are charged with repeatedly infringing the copyrights or other intellectual property rights of others.
